ABOUT OASIS

Oasis shot from obscurity to stardom in 1994, becoming one of Britain's most popular and critically acclaimed bands of the decade in the process. Along with and , they were responsible for returning British guitar pop to the top of the charts. Led by guitarist/songwriter , the Manchester quintet adopted the rough, thuggish image of and , crossed it with "" melodies and hooks, injected distinctly British lyrical themes and song structures like and , and tied it all together with a massive guitar roar, as well as a defiant sneer that drew equally from ' rebelliousness and ' cocksure arrogance. 's songs frequently reworked previous hits from ("Cigarettes and Alcohol" borrows the riff from "Bang a Gong") to ("Fade Away" takes the melody from "Freedom"), yet the group always put the hooks in different settings, updating past hits for a new era. Originally, the group was formed by schoolmates (vocals), Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs (guitar), Paul McGuigan (bass), and Tony McCaroll (drums). After spending several years as the guitar technician for a -inspired group named , returned to Manchester to find that his brother had formed a band. agreed to join if he could have complete control of the group, including contributing all the songs; the rest of the band agreed and adopted a new name, Oasis, before launching a year of intensive rehearsals.

After playing a handful of small club gigs, the band cornered Alan McGee, the head of Creation Records, and forced him to listen to their demo. Impressed, he signed the band and helped them ready their debut album. The group released their first single, "Supersonic," in the spring of 1994; it edged its way into the charts on the back of positive reviews. With a melody adapted from "I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing," "Shakermaker" became a bigger hit in the early summer. Released a month before their debut album's arrival, the soaring ballad "Live Forever" became a major hit in England and helped make Definitely Maybe the fastest-selling debut in British history. The record entered the charts at number one and eventually sold over seven million copies. Oasis mania continued throughout 1994, as the group began playing larger theaters and watched each new single outperform the last. However, tensions in the group began to build -- and refused to do joint interviews because they always fought -- and briefly left the band at the end of a difficult fall American tour. However, he quickly re-joined and the band headed back to England. As "Supersonic" began to climb the U.S. album rock and modern rock charts, the string-laden "Whatever" (a non-album single) hit number two over the British Christmas season.

MEMBERS OF THE BAND

LIAM GALLAGHER

William John Paul Gallagher born 21 September 1972 is an English singer and songwriter. One of the most recognisable figures in British rock music, Gallagher is noted for his distinctive vocal style and outspoken, arrogant, and volatile personality.

NOEL GALLAGHER

Noel Thomas David Gallagher born 29 May 1967 is an English guitarist, singer and songwriter. He was the chief songwriter, lead guitarist and co-lead vocalist of band until their split in 2009. He is one of the most successful songwriters in British music history, as the writer of eight UK number-one singles and co-writer of a further number one.

Paul Benjamin Arthurs (born 23 June 1965), known professionally as Bonehead, is an English musician. He is best known as the rhythm guitarist, occasional keyboardist and co-founder of the rock band Oasis.

Paul Francis McGuigan (born 9 May 1971), known professionally as Guigsy (pronounced "Gwigzee"), is an English musician. He is best known as the bassist and co-founder of the rock band Oasis.

Anthony McCarroll (born 4 June 1971) is an English drummer and one of the founding members of the English rock band Oasis, as their drummer from 1991 until his dismissal in April 1995.
